Gauge My Level

Gauge My Level explains how the XP bar and levels on the home screen relate to learning progress.

  • Level 1 – Getting started: beginning to recognise everyday risks.
  • Level 2 – Building confidence: applying advice in real situations.
  • Level 3 – Independent user: spotting most suspicious activity independently.
  • Level 4 – Emerging specialist: ready for more technical study or projects.

In the full build, this page would host an interactive diagnostic quiz that awards XP automatically.

Certification Hub

Browse example routes into cyber security. Choose a level to see more detail.

Routes shown here are examples only and will differ by country and provider.

🎓1️⃣📘

Beginner certifications

Suitable for learners who are new to cyber security and want to build confidence with core digital safety skills.

  • Entry‑level digital skills or IT user qualifications.
  • Short online courses about staying safe online and recognising scams.
  • Badges or awards offered by schools, colleges or youth organisations.

These courses focus on safe everyday behaviour rather than deep technical topics.

🎓2️⃣📙

Intermediate certifications

For learners who already understand the basics and want to explore technical ideas such as networking, coding and systems.

  • College or sixth‑form pathways that combine networking, programming and security.
  • Vendor‑neutral certificates that introduce general cyber security principles.
  • Project‑based programmes where learners investigate real‑world case studies.

These routes often prepare learners for apprenticeships or higher‑level study.

🎓3️⃣📗

Advanced certifications

For learners considering cyber security as a career and ready for in‑depth technical work.

  • Specialist degrees or higher apprenticeships focusing on cyber security.
  • Professional exams linked to roles such as security analyst or penetration tester.
  • Programmes that include workplace experience or simulated incident response.

Learners should discuss options with a tutor or careers adviser before choosing an advanced route.

Example: Phishing

Fraudulent messages that pretend to be from a trusted source, often asking you to click a link or confirm details.

Privacy Policy

How we protect your data

This prototype is designed for classroom use and stores data locally in the browser. It does not send information to a remote server.

What data do we collect?

  • Basic profile information such as an email address.
  • Progress data such as XP and level.
  • Locally‑saved preferences for learning activities.

Your control

You can clear your browser storage at any time to remove the data created by this prototype. Any real deployment would provide fuller options and contact details.
